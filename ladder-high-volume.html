Company Overview

  • Earnings Date

    Definition: The date the company reports earnings.

    Why it matters: Avoid selling options near earnings because price becomes unpredictable.

  • Market Cap

    Definition: Total value of the company.

    Why it matters: Larger companies tend to be more stable.

  • Free Cash Flow

    Definition: Cash left after expenses and investments.

    Why it matters: Positive FCF means the company generates real cash.

  • Gross Profit Margin

    Definition: Percentage of revenue left after cost of goods.

    Why it matters: Shows pricing power and product strength.

  • Operating Margin

    Definition: Profit after operating expenses.

    Why it matters: Shows business efficiency.

  • Net Profit Margin

    Definition: Profit after all expenses.

    Why it matters: Shows true profitability.

  • Financial Leverage

    Definition: How much debt the company uses.

    Why it matters: Lower or declining leverage = healthier balance sheet.

  • Quick Ratio

    Definition: Ability to pay short-term bills using liquid assets.

    Why it matters: Higher = safer.

  • Current Ratio

    Definition: Ability to cover short-term obligations.

    Why it matters: Shows liquidity strength.

Graph of Company

  • Stock Price Trend

    Definition: Direction of the stock (uptrend/downtrend).

    Why it matters: Uptrend = safer CSPs; downtrend = higher risk.

  • RSI

    Definition: Momentum indicator (0–100). Ideal CSP zone: RSI 40–50.

    Why it matters: Shows a pullback without being oversold.

  • Fibonacci Retracements

    Definition: Support and resistance levels based on price waves.

    Why it matters: Helps identify safer CSP strike zones.

  • Delta Selection Using Fibonacci Levels

    Definition: Use the stock’s position relative to Fibonacci retracement levels to choose the safest delta for your CSP strike.

    Rule:

    • Price ABOVE Fibonacci levels: The chart is strong. It is safer to use a higher delta (20–24) because support levels sit above your strike.
    • Price BELOW Fibonacci levels: The chart is weak. Use a lower delta (10–16) because support has failed and downside continuation is more likely.
    • Price BETWEEN Fibonacci levels: Neutral structure. Use a mid-range delta (14–20).

    Why it matters: Fibonacci levels act as natural support zones. When price is above them, pullbacks tend to bounce early. When price is below them, the trend is broken and risk increases.

  • IV / Implied Volatility %

    Definition: Expected future volatility.

    Why it matters: Higher IV = higher premium; low IV = weak premium.

Business Model

  • 1. Understand the Business

    Definition: Buffett only invests in businesses he fully understands. If you cannot explain a company’s business model in simple terms and know exactly how it makes money, you should pass.

    Why it matters: Stick to industries where you can reasonably predict demand and earnings 5–10 years into the future.

  • 2. Look for a Durable Moat

    Definition: A great business needs a protective “moat” that keeps competitors away and maintains pricing power.

    Examples of moats:

    • Brand power — recognizable brands consumers trust globally
    • High switching costs
    • Network effects
    • Cost advantages

CSP Checklist

  • Confirm Total Capital Available

    Definition: Cash available to sell puts.

  • Set Target Deployment

    Definition: Percentage of capital you plan to use this week.

  • Confirm Delta Target (≤ 17)

    Definition: Delta measures probability of finishing ITM.

    Why it matters: Lower delta = safer CSP.

  • OTM > 79%

    Definition: Probability the option expires worthless.

    Why it matters: Higher % = safer trade.

  • DTE (7–12 days or your preference)

    Definition: Days until expiration.

    Why it matters: Shorter DTE = faster premium decay.

  • RSI 40–50

    Definition: Pullback zone for safer entries.

  • IV Check

    Definition: Higher IV = higher premium.

  • Avoid Earnings

    Definition: Do not sell CSPs into earnings.

  • Liquidity Check

    Definition: Tight bid/ask + good volume.

  • Contingent Order (How-To)

    Definition: Auto-trigger order based on stock price.

Possible ITM

  • Delta Increase

    Definition: Higher delta = higher assignment risk.

    Rule: If delta rises above your threshold (ex: 0.28–0.30), evaluate action.

  • Theta Decrease

    Definition: Theta = daily premium decay.

    Rule: If theta drops too low, the trade is no longer paying you.

  • Close Position

    Definition: Exit the trade.

    Use when: You want capital back or risk is rising.

  • Roll Position

    Definition: Move to a later expiration or lower strike.

    Use when: You want to stay in the trade.

  • Assignment Plan

    Definition: If assigned, you now own shares.

    Next step: Move to covered calls.

Weekly Routine

  • Sunday Night Prep

    Definition: Review fundamentals, trend, RSI, IV, and earnings.

    Why it matters: Helps build your watchlist for the week.

  • Monday Morning Setup

    Definition: Sell CSPs 15–45 minutes after market open.

    Why it matters: Best fills and best premium.

  • Mid-Week Check

    Definition: Review delta, theta, and price trend.

    Why it matters: Early warning for ITM risk.

  • Friday Review

    Definition: Close winners early if most premium is gone.

    Why it matters: Frees up capital for next week.

Best Timing Rules

  • Best Day to Sell CSPs

    Definition: Monday or Tuesday.

    Why it matters: Captures the most theta decay.

  • Best Time of Day

    Definition: 15–45 minutes after market open.

    Why it matters: Spreads tighten and IV stabilizes.

  • Avoid Macro Event Days

    Definition: CPI, FOMC, Jobs Report.

    Why it matters: Price becomes unpredictable.

  • Avoid Fridays

    Definition: Premium collapses late in the week.

    Why it matters: You take weekend risk for little reward.
